Introduction/Overview

The Ideal Gas Law is one of the fundamental concepts in physics and physical chemistry, which describes the behavior of an idealized gas under changing environmental conditions, which include temperature, pressure, the volume of the gas, and the mass of the gas. First, let's define these terms:


  • Temperature:    The average kinetic energy of the gas particles. This of this as the "speed" of the gas
  • Pressure:    The force a gas exerts on its container. Think of this as the frequency with which the particles hit the site of the container
  • Volume:    The volume that the gas occupies. If the gas is in a rigid container, this is equal to the container's volume
  • Mass:    I could explain this a few different ways, but for our purposes think of it as the number of particles in our gas sample



The actual relationship of all these variables can be expressed as:

PV = nRT


Where:

  • P equals the pressure of the gas in atmospheres (atm)
  • V equals the volume of the gas in liters (L)
  • n equals the mass of the gas in moles (m)
  • R equals a constant equal to 0.0821 (don't worry about units)
  • T equals the temperature of the gas in Kelvin (K)
